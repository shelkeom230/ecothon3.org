What is Ecothon?

ECOTHON is a kind of hackathon where you have to create models or software using various technologies which can benefit mother nature in multiple ways.

  • ECOTHON will be an online-offline(hybrid) hackathon.
  • Authentic and unique projects which satisfy the criteria are to be accepted.
  • The top three winners will be decided at the end of the second rounds.
    • Round 1: Top 25 out of all teams will be selected by the Ecothon Team.
    • Round 2: Final round. The top 3 winners will be declared.
      • The top 3 winners will get fixed prize money, and deserving projects will get rewards.
      • Also, the Consolation prize will be given to the deserving project.

Problem Statements

1. Agriculture And Farming

1. Seed quality analysis through Multispectral imaging.
Description :
Post-harvesting seeds and their optimum germination in another cycle are key to success in farming. Seed quality can change due to several reasons. These can be physical or physiological. Identifying these and mitigating them can improve the success of farming, including overcoming seed infections.
2. Soil nutrient analysis.
Description :
A systematic evaluation of all the factors and identifying solutions with new technologies will help in successful agricultural practices.
3. Rapid detection of bacterial and fungal infections in plants.
Description :
Infections can differ during various stages of growth and at different parts of the plants. These will have a significant impact on crop yield. Identifying microbial infection early that can destroy the plants will facilitate its eradication for better yield.
4. To create a system that is based on a prediction model and can predict the weather and rain forecasting. for society..
Description :
Farmers face a lot of problems with the production of their crops because of uncertainty in weather. Prediction tasks may help them in a direct or indirect manner, like weather forecasting, rain prediction, etc. The prediction capability of the system should be proper and should be able to identify rain predictions that should not be taken from soil moisture.
5. Precision agriculture, including applications of sensors, WSN, ICT, AI, IoT, & Drone.
Description :
Monitoring and management of water quality, soil fertility, and health, crop- growth and health (pest diseases, macro and micronutrients, water). Precision livestock farming and aquaculture.
6. Supply chain and agriculture logistics.
Description :
Blockchain technology for traceability of agri-produce from farm to fork. Cost-effective sensor-based real-time monitoring and management of storage and transport systems. IT-based agriculture logistics, including custom hiring services.
7. Post-harvest food technology and value addition.
Description :
Development of devices for assessing quality, grading, sorting and processing of agri-produce. Development of biodegradable and smart packaging materials. Environment-friendly cost-effective mobile technologies at the farm gate for processing and value addition.

2. Forest / Wildlife Conservation

1. Android App for Geo-tagging location of wildlife and forest offense and sighting of wildlife in the forest areas.
Description :
Indian forests have a fragile ecosystem with vivid flora and fauna. Protecting wildlife is critical to its environment. An increase in wildlife offenses like the killing of protected animals has increased in the recent past. This app will enable us to curb such offenses.
2. Improving access of local NGOs to conservation funding.
Description :
Despite the increased recognition and involvement of NGOs in sustainable development generally, and in the conservation of threatened species specifically, a major handicap remains their access to funds for attending training and conferences, funds for conducting research, advocacy, and behavior change activities within local communities and with other stakeholders involved in activities that threaten biodiversity.
3. Identifying camera trap captures and remotely sending data.
Description :
Motion-triggered cameras (also called camera traps) can provide invaluable evidence of wildlife movements and behavior without the influence of human or vehicular presence. Camera traps can also be used to monitor human movement on less frequently trafficked animal paths or tracks. While this human movement is often benign, motion-triggered cameras located on animal tracks and paths can capture photographic evidence of individuals carrying weapons, materials for traps, or other paraphernalia related to wildlife and wild lands crime.
4. Report an injured animal or bird.
Description :
Detection of an injured animal so that we can contact the animal helpline to report an injured bird or animal.




5. Mitigating Human-Wildlife Conflict.
Description :
The challenge is to develop tools and models for managing multiple lands uses including real-time monitoring and mapping of potential/actual conflict zones and species, in order to develop innovative policy, technical and technological solutions to target and mitigate the various drivers, forms and consequences of human-wildlife conflicts.
6. Preventing livestock depredation at pasture by large predators.
Description :
You could consider a livestock tracking device that would allow herders and support teams to locate lost livestock quickly.
7. Profiling and Combating Zoonotic Disease Risk.
Description :
A decision-making tool Identify, manage, and reduce the risk of zoonotic diseases, from wildlife trafficking and consumption, wildlife markets, or human encroachment into wildlife habitat.

3. Renewable/Sustainable energy

1. Solutions could be in the form of waste segregation, disposal, and improve sanitization system.
Description :
Electrical energy storage (EES) is expected to become as important for electric grids and for electric vehicle traction as it is today for portable electronic devices. Meanwhile, lithium-ion batteries are dominating all applications in portable electronic devices. Electrical energy storage (EES) is expected to become as important for electric grids and for electric vehicle traction as it is today for portable electronic devices. Meanwhile, lithium-ion batteries are dominating all applications in portable electronic devices.
2. Street Solar Power System.
Description :
The Government took a bold step to promote the utilization of Solar Power by electrifying the streets using solar power. However, it has been found that the street solar power system works less efficiently and their life is relatively lesser in comparison to solar power at homes. This could be due to the lack of proper protection of the batteries and environmental effects. What innovative ideas can be incorporated into these systems for their better efficiency and long life?
3. Smart Networks for Renewable Integrated Sources for standalone Microgrids.
Description :
Conditioning Monitoring (CM) of an electrical system is the process of systematic data collection and evaluation to identify changes in performance or conditioning of the system, or its components, such that remedial action may be planned in a cost-effective manner to maintain reliability. The appropriate generic tools having low cost providing safe solutions are required to address the challenge for robust and reliable micro grid
5. Floating Solar.
Description :
Floating solar is the solar PV array on a structure that is floating on a body of water and it has huge potential in large hydropower dams. However, with the installation of large floating solar PV systems, aquatic lives are affected somehow. The structure prevents sunlight from entering the surface of the water. Your idea should explore the potential of floating solar PV systems in Nepal as well as accommodate the system without affecting aquatic lives.
4. Energy Conservation with modern technology.
Description :
The world’s natural resources are already depleting, and people are starting to feel the repercussions of a wasteful lifestyle. As such, energy conservation is vital in today’s world. Not only does it help households save money, but it also encourages consumers to be more conscious about preserving natural resources. IoT has made homes smarter, so its application in energy conservation is more accessible. Smart appliances can inform homeowners of real-time consumption so that they can monitor their energy usage. Reports can easily be generated or extracted, allowing homeowners to monitor their daily, weekly, and monthly energy consumption accurately. In addition, sensors can be installed so that when owners leave the house, appliances will automatically turn off to conserve energy. Summary: In order to conserve energy we need to keep a check on all the type of resources that are being used in our day to day life. With IoT it becomes easy to track the resources we are using and we can monitor and keep a check on. Reports generated from such monitoring system can help in conserving natural resources and reduce unnecessary wastage if any. Objective: : We would like you to develop a mobile/web app system using IoT to make energy conservation easy. Also provide reports on usage of all the resources in day to day life and smart automation methods to reduce the amounts of any resource we use.
6. Cost of Air Pollution to Solar Power Generation (CAPsolar).
Description :
"Emerging economies of Asia are looking to Solar power generation as key to their independance from imported energy resources. Even for fossil fuel-rich countries, climate sensitivity and targets are motivation to switch to alternate energy resources such as solar power. This ratio is poised to get further improve due to rollout of electric vehicles. Solar power genertaion capacity of a country depends on many factors. Since Asian countries such as India and ASEAN are within tropics, the day length is not a major concern for any part of the year. With governments facilitating rooftop solar model within the grids, gigentic areas for solar panel installation aren't mendatory. This leaves the air quality (haze) that determines what wavelengths and intensity of sunlight is received at ground level. Air pollutants have many sources, natural as well as anthropogenic. For both, the source need not to be near same place or even country. Massive duststorms or wildfires have been known to spread the haze across the continent. Due to solar power installation being at the base of it's exponential, it is not yet clear if and at what scale the air quality impacts on solar power generation. Solar insolation data and climatology (long-term averages) at global scale is available. Air quality data is available from satellite platforms such as NASA-MODIS series satellites on daily scale. This satellite data can be processed with open access software. A solution would look into available solar power generation data and correlate it with parameters such as Aerosol Optical Depth, to estimate the impact of AOD to solar power generation capacity. This can further be expressed in terms of electricity costing, to put a price tag that a plant has to pay due to bad air quality. Dataset- https://oceancolor.gsfc.nasa.gov/"
7. Scarcity of water in hilly areas during summer months..
Description :
"One of the major problems being faced in hill areas is severe scarcity of water during the summer months. Places, which are located in higher reaches and have witnessed rapid increase in population in the last few years often have to face severe shortage of water during summer months. At most places, water supply is received from natural underground water systems. With rapid urbanisation resulting in an increase in concrete surfaces, the water infiltration into ground during the rainy season has considerably decreased over the years. Thus, the natural sources often become insufficient to support the population, which is dependent on them. Large water tanks are often built in these places, where water is pumped from lower areas which are close to a river, lake etc. Water harvesting in these areas is a difficult task due to the rocky surface and restrictions related to blasting etc. A solution may be sought from students through this hackathon to solve the water problem."

4. Waste Management

1. Geo Tracking of Waste and Triggering Alerts and Mapping Areas with High Waste Index.
Description :
We do have waste lying in cities which makes it hard for cleaning staff to know which area requires attention and urgent garbage, waste pickup or Identifying Waste across in the City.
2. Gamification of SWM info for children.
Description :
Making learning Solid Waste Management fun for children.
3. Identify a spitter .
Description :
Identify a spitter. Let us support Swachh Bharat
4. Segregation of Waste.
Description :
Segregation of waste is now well known. But many confusions remain. A simple handy guide can help people to identify organic and recyclable waste.
5. Report non pick up of household waste.
Description :
Nowadays, waste is collected from houses through tippers. But often there are issues of non-collection of waste which result in grievances and also such households throwing waste outside. A simple app to identify left houses so that remedial action can be taken.
6. Dealing with Garbage Vulnerable points.
Description :
Garbage Vulnerable Points are points in a city where people throw garbage. We need to identify and manage these points.
7. To develop an ONLINE ecofriendly mechanism to dispose of various types of waste material which can be converted into energy..
Description :
The most common technology for waste to energy conversion is incineration. In this process, the organics collected from the waste has burnt at a high temperature. This type of treatment is called thermal treatment.
